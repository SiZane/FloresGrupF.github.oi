CRITICALLY ENDANGERED & ENDANGERED

ANIMAL

Siberian Tiger (Panthera Tigris Altaica)

A subspecies of the tiger which is also the largest cat in the world.
Length: 3.3 m (11 ft.)
Tail Length: 1 m (3 ft.)
Weight: Males: ± 320 kg (700 lb.) Females: ± 180 kg (400 lb.)

Spread Range

These tigers live scattered in a small area in the southeastern region of Russia as well as in China and North Korea, although in small numbers.

Causes of Population Decline

Main Causes: Poaching (They are hunted only for trophies and ingredients in traditional Chinese medicine, although poaching has decreased, it is still a major threat to these magnificent tigers.) and forest destruction.

Javan Rhinoceros (Rhinoceros Sondaicus)

This rhino is one of two rhinos that are native to Indonesia.
Body Length: ± 2 - 4 m (6 – 11 ft)
Single Horn Length: ± 25.4 cm (10 in.)
However, the females have shorter horns or even no horns at all.
Body Weight: 900 – 2300 kg (2000 – 5090 lb.)

Spread Range

In the past, the Javan rhino lived scattered from northeastern India and southeast Asia, especially the mountains of West Java at an altitude of 3,000 meters above sea level.
Unfortunately, due to poaching the Javan Rhino population has declined and now there are only a few dozen left in Ujung Kulon National Park, Banten, Indonesia.

Causes of Population Decline

Initially, the main cause of the decline in the Javan rhino population was poaching. However, the biggest threat currently to the Javan Rhino is inbreeding which causes a decline in quality and weakens the rhino's ability to cope with disease or natural disasters.
This often happens because the rhino population only lives in a national park area that is small enough for them.

Tapanuli Orangutan (Pongo Tapanuliensis)

Since 1997 this orangutan species was discovered, but it was only in 2017 that this orangutan species was designated as a different species from the Sumatran orangutan after several genetic and morphological studies.
Height: Male: 137cm (54 inches). Female: 110 cm (43 inches).
Body Weight: Males: ± 70-90 kg (150-200 lb.). Females: ± 40-50 kg (88-110 lb.).
Distinguishing features: Differently shaped and smaller skull, thicker and matted coat, presence of mustache and beard, and flat cheek pads covered with a light layer of fine blond hair.

Spread Range

Since it was first discovered, the Tapanuli Orangutan population has only remained in an area called Batang Toru, south of Lake Toba, Sumatra Island.

Causes of Population Decline

Main Cause: Loss of habitat, due to deforestation for settlements, infrastructure projects, and oil palm plantations. As well as illegal hunting, to make baby orangutans as pets.

Amur Leopard (Panthera Pardus Orientalis)

This leopard is different from the others. This leopard does not live in the savanna, but in temperate forests.
Body Length: ± 107–136 cm (42–54 inches). Tail Length: ± 82–90 cm (32–35 inches)
Body Weight: Males: ± 32.2–48 kg (71–106 lb). Females: ± 25–42.5 kg (55–94 lb).
The Amur Leopard is also known as the “Silent Killer” because this leopard is very good at sneaking up on its prey.

Spread Range

The Amur Leopard is named after the Amur River, a river that separates Eastern Russia and North China, the mountainous forests where they can be found. Amur Leopard can also be found in North Korea

Causes of Population Decline

Main Causes : Poaching, logging for agriculture, coal mines and oil pipelines are a threat to these big cats. Not only that, inbreeding is also a serious threat.

PLANT

Javanese Edelweiss (Anaphalis Javanica)

This flower is known as a perennial flower because its age is estimated to reach more than 100 years. This is because the edelweiss flower contains the hormone ethylene which functions to prevent this flower from falling.
This plant can grow to less than 1 meter, but some can grow up to 8 meters.
It has sharp leaves with a length of 4-6 cm.
Has 5-6 flower heads measuring about 5 mm on each flower stalk.

Spread Range

Javanese edelweiss are found in the mountainous areas of Java, Southern Sumatra, Southern Sulawesi, and Lombok at an altitude of 1,600 - 3,600 meters above sea level. This plant is able to grow alive on the barren slopes of former fires, as well as in open areas such as peaks and craters.

Causes of Population Decline

The number of these plants is decreasing due to the ignorance of tourists who pluck carelessly because they are captivated by the beauty of this flower. There are even some people who sell them as souvenirs. The good news is that this flower is protected by law in Indonesia.

Baseball Plant (Euphorbia Obesa)

E. Obesa is an interesting plant, this plant has a round shape like a ball and has no thorns, so it is suitable for decoration.
This plant has a diameter of 6 – 15 cm depending on its age, and its shape even changes to become more cylindrical with age.
This plant has such a name because it has 8 longitudinal ribs with protrusions on the edges that make it look like a baseball.
E. Obesa is almost the same as a cactus, they store water in their stems so that this plant is drought tolerant, it's just that E. Obesa doesn't have thorns like a cactus.

Spread Range

E. Obesa is an endemic plant from the Great Karoo region, South Africa. This plant lives in sandy, very rocky and hilly soil areas and with summer rainfall of around 200-300 mm per year. E. Obesa usually grows at an altitude of 300 – 900 meters above sea level.

Causes of Population Decline

Its shape is similar to a baseball, causing many baseball fans to take and collect it excessively as a collection, so that the population becomes severely damaged and even endangered. Therefore, this plant is now protected by the South African government. Not only that, the slow growth and the small number of seeds which only amounted to 2-3 in each pod played a role in the decline in the E. Obesa population.

Welwitschia Mirabilis

Welwitschia Mirabilis may look like a dead plant, in fact it can even survive in the desert.
This unique plant is also called "Living Fossil", not only because it is estimated to have existed for thousands of years, this plant can also survive for a very long time, even more than 1,000 years.
This plant is very unusual because it only consists of the base of the stem, roots and leaves full of stomata that continue to elongate with an average growth of 8-15 cm per year. Although the longer it grows, the more wrinkled and withered the leaves become. Because of the wind and heat, the leaves of this plant are split so that they look many instead of two.
Just like the leaves, the roots of this plant grow very long, up to 30 m in search of water.

Spread Range

This plant, discovered by Friedrich Welwitsch, lives scattered on the coast of Namibia and Angola, as well as the Namib Desert, which is probably the oldest desert in the world.

Causes of Population Decline

Even though this plant can last for years, climate change is making the earth warmer and this will threaten the survival of this unique plant. Coupled with the development of land that occurs.

Snowdonia Hawkweed (Hieraciumm Snowdoniense)

In 1957, this plant was believed to be extinct, but in 2002, someone accidentally found a plant of this species on a remote hillside which is part of the Cwm Idwal National Nature Reserve.
But in fact, after disappearing for years, this plant reappeared not only one but 3.
In 2021, the number of these plants is likely to have doubled to six, but due to the difficulty in accessing where these plants are located, it is not certain that these plants are the mysterious Hieracium Snowdoniense species.
The flowers of this species are beautiful bright yellow in color, complete with a small rosette of leaves that extend at the base, growing at the end of a fine stalk covered with tiny black hairs.
These plants, which can grow up to ± 30 cm (12 inches), apparently also produce fruit, although not regularly, and will ripen after about one month.

Spread Range

The last three specimens were found on a cliff near Bethesda, a town on the outskirts of Snowdonia. Yes, the name shows where it came from, this plant is endemic and spread in the mountains of Snowdonia, North Wales, United Kingdom.

Causes of Population Decline

After being presumed extinct for years, many experts believe the main reason for the decline in the population of this plant is due to long-term overgrazing of local sheep. Fortunately, in the early 2000s, a rescue team collected seeds to plant in the National Botanic Garden of Wales. Now, the seeds have flowered in that place.
